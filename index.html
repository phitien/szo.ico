Vision of SOZO

To create a global, decentralized, block-chain-based, Artificial intelligence-driven food retail ecosystem via online as well as offline platform that provides highly-efficient data-driven supply chain management and PoS system without intermediaries.

Problems in the industry

Total Value of Global Food and Agricultural Industry amounts to 8.1 trillion in 2017 and continues to soar with the tremendous increase of population growth.  World population is expected to reach nearly 10 billion by 2050. Increasing by 33% from an estimated 7.6 billion now.

FOOD SUPPLYCHAIN INEFFICIENCIES

Supply chain inefficiencies in the food industry pose a great challenge, adding much unnecessary cost to the final price causing high inflation of food product.
Eg; Fruit & Vegetables were wasted by 492 million tons worldwide due to the inefficient and ineffective food supply chain management.

FOOD SAFETY

Existing food marketplace model lacks transparency & traceability to prove the quality of food is good enough for consumption. Food safety problems can be quite costly.
Eg; An E. coli outbreak in Germany caused $1.3 billion in losses to the global agricultural industry.

FOOD FRAUD & ADULTERATION

Current food safety system is not advanced enough for fraud mitigation. Beyond the economic cost, food fraud can harm public health and damage consumer trust.
Eg; Nearly 70% of the milk in India is adulterated. India government is developing new regulatory, legal framework and food safety administration.

Why we focus on food

FAO estimates that 1.02 billion people are undernourished worldwide in 2009. These are more hungry people than at any time since 1970, the earliest year for which comparable statistics are available.
Hunger has increased not as a result of poor harvests but because of high domestic food prices, lower incomes and increasing unemployment due to the global economic crisis. Many poor people cannot afford to buy the food they need. The chart shows where the world’s hungry people live.

Other challenges for food indusry

High inflation rate in many countries makes it more difficult for people to afford the basic necessities if their labour is unable to keep pace with the inflation rate. This causes families to struggle as they attempt to keep up with the price of everything from cornflakes to college tuition.
In many countries, high farming cost with high inflation rate results in high domestic food prices that affect people with lower income. Many poor people cannot afford to buy the food they need. Hunger is the direct outcome from this serious issue
Ineffective, costly and outdated trade promotions practice. Trade promotion spending represent 17% of manufacturer’s sales . Each year, over $50 billion on trade promotions never reaches the consumer. Unfairness in today’s promotion-laden atmosphere go hand in hand with the rising costs of promotions and the inefficiencies they produce.
